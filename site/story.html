Why we built this

Helping a parent with their Medicare drug plan shouldn't feel like dodging salespeople.

It usually starts at a kitchen table. A parent is worried about the cost of their prescriptions, the enrollment window is open, and the person helping — often an adult child on their phone — goes looking for a straight answer.

What they find instead is a wall of lead-generation sites dressed up to look official: countdown timers, “licensed agents standing by,” phone numbers that lead to a sales pitch, and forms that harvest your information the moment you type a drug name. Nearly every Medicare website is trying to sell you something — or sell you.

PlanRobin is the opposite of that. It takes the public data Medicare already publishes — the same formulary and pricing files the plans file with CMS — and just shows you the math: what your medications would actually cost across every plan in your county, with the reasoning in plain sight.

What PlanRobin is — and isn't

Free, and always will be.
Anonymous. Your medication list stays on your device; no accounts, no logins, nothing saved.
Non-commercial. We don't sell insurance, take commissions, or sell your data. There is nothing to buy here.
Independent. A private website — not affiliated with the federal Medicare program or any insurer.
Educational, not advice. We show estimates from public data; always confirm a plan on Medicare.gov or with a counselor before enrolling.
Want a human in your corner? Free, unbiased Medicare counseling — no sales pitch — is available from your State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP). Find a counselor → or call 1-800-MEDICARE.
